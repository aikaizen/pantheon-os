# Consultancy-to-Product Growth Strategy

## Purpose
This document outlines how an AI/agent consultancy can evolve from high-touch services into a repeatable product business without losing cash flow, client trust, or delivery quality.

## Core thesis
The consultancy should not try to jump directly from custom work to a standalone software company. It should use services as the discovery and distribution engine for product creation.

The right sequence is:
1. sell high-value advisory and implementation work
2. extract repeated workflows, data structures, and operator interfaces from delivery
3. standardize those repeatable pieces into internal tools
4. package the highest-leverage internal tool as a product wedge
5. shift revenue mix from one-off projects to recurring software and managed-service subscriptions

## Ideal customer profile
Best-fit buyers:
- founder-led or operator-led firms with revenue between $1M and $20M
- agencies, consultancies, professional services firms, B2B operators, and internal transformation teams
- organizations with multi-step, delay-prone workflows
- teams already paying for execution overhead, coordination, reporting, and follow-up failure

Poor-fit buyers:
- companies looking for speculative innovation with no operational pain
- teams without process ownership
- buyers that want a custom AI science project instead of a measurable business outcome

## Positioning
Position the company as:
- an execution systems consultancy first
- a workflow and memory infrastructure company second
- a generalized AI platform company much later

Message:
- we reduce operational drag in multi-step work
- we make teams more reliable, not just more automated
- we turn messy human + software workflows into managed systems with visibility, memory, and follow-through

## Offer ladder
### 1. Diagnostic / strategy sprint
Deliverables:
- workflow audit
- bottleneck map
- automation opportunities
- ROI model
- recommended 90-day plan

### 2. Pilot implementation
Deliverables:
- one workflow or operating loop improved end-to-end
- measurable time savings or throughput lift
- dashboard, memory, and workflow instrumentation

### 3. Managed operating retainer
Deliverables:
- ongoing optimization
- recurring reporting
- workflow maintenance
- human-in-the-loop approvals
- monthly roadmap updates

### 4. Productized module subscription
Potential modules:
- institutional memory engine
- long-horizon workflow agent
- runtime control plane

## Product transition model
### What to productize
Only productize assets that meet all three conditions:
- repeated across multiple clients
- hard to operate manually at scale
- valuable enough to justify ongoing subscription spend

### What to keep as services
Keep high-context work as services:
- executive strategy
- custom systems design
- change management
- onboarding and rollout
- client-specific integrations

### Revenue mix target
Suggested transition path:
- phase 1: 80% services / 20% recurring
- phase 2: 60% services / 40% recurring
- phase 3: 35% services / 65% recurring

The goal is not to eliminate services immediately. The goal is to make services feed the product roadmap and become higher-margin advisory work over time.

## Product portfolio recommendation
Recommended product sequence:
1. Institutional Memory Engine
   - fastest path to repeatability across clients
   - immediate value in every consultancy delivery context
2. Long-Horizon Workflow Agent
   - strongest wedge for workflow execution and multi-week coordination
3. Agent Runtime Control Plane
   - higher-complexity control surface for clients operating multiple agents and automations

## Go-to-market strategy
### Initial GTM motion
Use services as the acquisition channel.

Primary motions:
- founder-led outbound to known operators
- thought leadership around workflow reliability, agent operations, and institutional memory
- diagnostic offer as low-friction entry point
- case-study-driven expansion

### Sales narrative
Do not sell “AI.” Sell:
- missed follow-up reduced
- reporting time reduced
- project drift reduced
- reuse of institutional knowledge increased
- throughput increased without proportional headcount growth

### Expansion path
Land with one workflow, then expand through:
- adjacent teams
- additional workflows
- recurring governance/reporting
- product module adoption

## Operating model
### Team structure
Near-term team shape:
- founder / principal operator
- solutions architect
- workflow engineer / automations lead
- delivery manager
- part-time design/research support

### Delivery loop
1. discover workflow pain
2. map workflow and knowledge dependencies
3. implement pilot
4. measure results
5. extract reusable components
6. convert the reusable component into product surface

### Product rule
Anything rebuilt three times should be reviewed for productization.

## Commercial model
### Services pricing
- diagnostic sprint: fixed fee
- pilot: fixed fee + scoped success criteria
- retainer: monthly recurring

### Product pricing
Use value-based subscription pricing tied to:
- number of workflows
- number of users/operators
- number of active automations/agents
- memory volume or workspace count

## Success metrics
Track four categories:
### Revenue
- services revenue
- recurring software revenue
- revenue mix
- gross margin by line

### Product traction
- pilot-to-subscription conversion rate
- number of active productized modules in use
- weekly active operator teams

### Delivery leverage
- reusable component rate
- average time to deploy a new client workflow
- hours saved per engagement

### Strategic quality
- number of repeatable use cases discovered
- number of workflows with measurable ROI
- number of client requests satisfied by existing product surface versus new custom work

## Key risks
### Risk: becoming a custom shop forever
Mitigation:
- explicitly review reuse after every project
- maintain a product roadmap independent of client whims
- reject low-reuse work unless it is strategically valuable

### Risk: building a product too early
Mitigation:
- do not productize before repeated demand exists
- use services to validate price and urgency first

### Risk: weak differentiation
Mitigation:
- focus on workflow reliability, memory, and operator control rather than generic AI tooling
- choose a concrete wedge with measurable outcomes

### Risk: operational sprawl
Mitigation:
- standardize delivery templates
- maintain a core data model across clients
- enforce an offer ladder instead of unlimited custom work

## 90-day execution plan
### Days 1-30
- define ICP and outreach thesis
- package diagnostic offer
- identify 3 to 5 target client problems
- create standard workflow-audit template

### Days 31-60
- close 1 to 2 pilot engagements
- measure repeated workflow and memory patterns
- build internal reusable assets
- decide first product wedge

### Days 61-90
- package the first productized module
- create one strong case study
- launch recurring retainer + module offer
- refine pricing and onboarding flow

## Strategic recommendation
For a consultancy-focused project, the most durable path is:
- services for discovery and revenue
- memory and workflow tooling for leverage
- product modules for scale

The company should optimize for repeatability, not novelty. The best products will emerge from the pain encountered repeatedly in delivery.
